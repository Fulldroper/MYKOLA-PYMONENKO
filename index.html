ABOUT
ARTIST
1862-1878
Mykola Kornylovych Pymonenko is a Ukrainian artist-painter, the author of many paintings on rural and urban themes, was born on March 9, 1862 in the picturesque suburb of Kyiv - Priortsi. His father was an icon painter and carver, he had a small workshop in which he made wood and painted iconostasis. Sometimes he had to go to the villages and towns of Kyiv region to fulfill orders; he took his son with him more than once on such trips.

ABROAD
1907
Since 1907, Mykola Pymonenko exhibited his works abroad - in Munich, Paris, Berlin, London, and in 1909 he was elected a member of the Paris International Union of Art and Literature. On art exhibition in Munich in 1906 with the picture was successfully exhibited "Holy Thursday" ("Exit from the church in Holy Thursday"). I bought the canvas the Munich Museum Neue Pinakoteke, the author was then elected a full member of the Munich Society of Painters. Paintings by M. Pymonenko exhibited at an exhibition in Rome.
